Our Story

A festival born from a vision

From a small idea in Kyiv to Georgia's biggest electronic gathering — Tbilisi Style 21 unites Georgian culture with world-class electronic music.

Hello, I'm Beka Abzianidze, born in Tbilisi. I first walked into a club at 17, and that night I knew this world was mine. From 2019 to early 2026 I lived in Kyiv, where I saw what a real festival could become.

The idea for Tbilisi Style 21 came one evening in Kyiv when a Ukrainian friend gifted me a hand-forged iron grape cluster. I lived on Vinogradar street then, and something clicked — Georgian wine, qvevri, electronic music. They belonged together.

Our mission is to fuse Georgian heritage with modern electronic sound, and to give Georgian talent the world stage they deserve. With your support, Tbilisi Style 21 can become what Tomorrowland, Ultra and Zamna already are — a destination festival that puts Georgia on the global map.

Food & Bars

Taste the festival

Three Food Zones inspired by traditional Georgian houses — chefs from Italy, Ukraine and Georgia, vegetarian and vegan options, late-night kitchen until festival closes.

Food Zones are designed in the style of old Georgian architecture — wooden ode houses, open kitchens, and live cooking that turns dining into an experience.

Chefs from three countries serve fast, modern menus tuned to festival tempo. Cashless payment everywhere, short queues, and a late-night kitchen that runs until the last beat drops.
